Uncovering Symmetric and Asymmetric Deterioration Patterns in Maryland’s Steel Bridges Through Time-Series Clustering and PCA - 2025

Peer-Reviewed Journal Publication | Symmetry (2025)

This study investigates long-term deterioration behavior in 1,378 Maryland steel bridges using Bridge Health Index (BHI) time-series data from 1995–2021.

By integrating K-means time-series clustering, bootstrap uncertainty analysis, and Principal Component Analysis (PCA), the research identifies six distinct deterioration archetypes and quantifies symmetric versus asymmetric structural degradation patterns across the statewide bridge network.

The findings reveal how traffic demand, span length, deck geometry, and rehabilitation history influence long-term structural performance. The framework provides a scalable, data-driven approach to support predictive asset management, inspection prioritization, and proactive infrastructure planning.

Authors: Soroush Piri | Zeinab Bandpey, PhD | Mehdi Shokouhian, PhD | Ruel Sabellano, PhD

Integrating Machine Learning Techniques for Enhanced Safety and Crime Analysis in Maryland - 2025

Peer-Reviewed Journal Publication | Applied Sciences (2025)

This study develops a comprehensive machine learning framework to analyze and predict crime trends across Maryland’s urban, suburban, and rural counties using multi-year crime and socio-economic datasets.

By integrating Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, XGBoost, Extra Trees, and advanced ensemble stacking models, the research identifies localized crime patterns, demographic influences, and temporal trends. The framework incorporates spatial and temporal analysis, feature engineering, and cross-validation to improve predictive accuracy and interpretability.

The results demonstrate how AI-driven predictive modeling can support data-informed policing strategies, hotspot detection, resource allocation, and equitable public safety planning across diverse regional contexts.

Authors: Soroush Piri | Zeinab Bandpey, PhD | Mehdi Shokouhian, PhD

Unravelling Safety Concerns in Construction: A Comprehensive Data Analysis - 2024

Peer-Reviewed Conference Publication | Associated Schools of Construction (ASC) (2024)

This study analyzes construction fatality reports from the NIOSH FACE program using text mining, statistical modeling, and network analysis to uncover patterns in accident causation. By transforming unstructured incident narratives into structured datasets, the research identifies dominant contributing factors, emerging safety trends, and relationships between root causes and recommended interventions. The framework demonstrates how AI-driven analytics can support proactive safety planning, targeted training programs, and data-informed decision-making in construction risk management.

Authors: Soroush Piri | Kamalesh Panthi, PhD

Exploring U.S. Occupant Perception Toward Indoor Air Quality Via Social Media and NLP Analysis - 2024

Peer-Reviewed Journal Publication | Journal of Environmental Science and Public Health (2024)

This nationwide study investigates U.S. occupant perception of indoor air quality (IAQ) during COVID-19 stay-at-home periods using geo-tagged social media data and Natural Language Processing (NLP). Using time-series sentiment analysis and emotion modeling in R, we analyzed over 45,000 tweets from 2019–2020 to evaluate shifts in public perception toward IAQ. The findings revealed increased negative sentiment during 2020, highlighting how prolonged indoor exposure influenced occupant dissatisfaction and building health awareness. The research demonstrates how AI-driven linguistic analysis can extract large-scale human insight to inform sustainable building design, ventilation strategies, and indoor environmental policy.

Authors: Mehdi Ashayeri, PhD | Soroush Piri | Narjes Abbasabadi, PhD

IntegrativeSentiment.io (iS.io) - 2022

A web-based interactive tool for integrated human sentiment modeling

iS.io is an interactive platform for analyzing large volumes of human feedback, such as social media text through real-time sentiment modeling. It combines NLP methods with emotion-oriented frameworks to translate raw text into interpretable indicators of sentiment.

The tool streamlines the full workflow (data collection, cleaning, tokenization, and modeling), so users can run analyses without needing to write code. It also supports network-style visualizations and map-based views to help users explore patterns across space and time.

Built in R and delivered through the URBiiLAB web portal, iS.io provides a browser-based experience that does not require working directly in RStudio.

Team: Mehdi Ashayeri, PhD (PI) | Soroush Piri (Graduate Assistant)
